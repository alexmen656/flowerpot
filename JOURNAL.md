# 1. CAD - around 2 hours
The CAD was pretty simple and straightforward

# 2. Putting everything together - 8 hours
This was the hardest part because of the many components I had to solder together. The speakers were very frustrating because they needed amplifiers, so I had to solder around 15 wires for each speaker. The rest wasn't that hard, just time consuming

# 3. Adding a Display - 3 hours
Initially I didn't plan to have a display, but then I decided later that it would be much cooler to have one - so I designed the display cover and added a 2.8 inch TFT screen

# 4. Idea for adding a LED strip
I also wanted to add a LED strip to the top, but the Pico didn't have enough power, so I didn't finish that.

# 5. Firmware - 2 hours
Firmware was a bit more complicated than I thought. TBH, AI assisted me with this step for like 50%, because the display added much more complexity with displaying a nice layout and icons. I used MicroPython for the firmware
